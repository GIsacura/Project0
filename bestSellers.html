The Institute; by Stephen King After intruders abduct Luke Ellis, he wakes up at The Institute in a windowless replica of his own room at home. He meets kids with telekinesis and telepathy, ruled over by the sinister Mrs. Sigsby. As time goes on, Luke becomes increasingly desperate to get away from The Institute. Problem is, no one ever has. 5
The Silent Patient; by Alex Michaelides After artist Alicia Berenson shoots her husband, she never speaks again. Her silence turns a domestic tragedy into a phenomenon, and Berenson quickly gains notoriety as a result. And when crime psychologist Theo Faber begins to work with Alicia, he's forced to examine his own motivations while he tries to uncover hers. 15
The Nickel Boys; by Colson Whitehead This riveting, appalling story is based on true accounts of abuses at a reform school for boys in Florida. The novel explores the echoes of the boys' childhood years later, as well as themes like race, honesty, friendship, and betrayal. It's a slim book you'll finish quickly, but won't forget in a hurry. 10
They Called Us Enemy; by George Takai In 1942, every person of Japanese descent on the West Coast got rounded up and shipped to one of 10 "relocation centers," including Takei, who was four years old at the time. This is the story of what it felt like to grow up under those circumstances, and how it set the stage for his future. 9
Red at the Bone; by Jacqueline Woodson When Melody's mother gets pregnant with her, two families from very different backgrounds become one. By moving backward and forward in time, this novel reveals how gentrification and racism, education and parenthood, and their shared history have affected everyone involved. 14
Say Nothing; by Patrick Radden Keefe In December 1972, Jean McConville, a mother of 10, was dragged from her Belfast home by masked intruders, never to be seen again. Her abduction was part of the vicious conflict known as The Troubles, which wracked Northern Ireland for decades. This searing book recounts the horrors of the time, as well as the devastating repercussions that followed. 14
The World That We Knew; by Alice Hoffman When Hanni Kohn sends her daughter Lea away to save her from the Nazi regime, a rabbi's daughter Ettie creates a mystical golem to protect her. Once the golem, Ava, comes to life, she becomes forever linked with Ettie and Lea. This is a story of loss, resistance, and everlasting love that intertwines the mortal with the fantastic. 12
Daisy Jones & The Six; by Taylor Jenkins Reid Turn the tunes on and get ready to rock. This history of a fictitious '70s rock band in their own words is juicy, emotional, and filled with powerful personalities that you'll love. Even if you're not a big music buff, this one will get you there. 20
The Whisper Man; by Alex North Tom Kennedy and his son Jake move to Featherbank for a fresh start after his wife's death, but the town has a dark past. Twenty years ago, "the Whisper Man" Frank Carter kidnapped and murdered five residents, luring them outside by whispering at their window. A young boy has disappeared in a way that echoes Carters' crimes, just as Tom and Jake get settled in. Then Jake starts hearing whispers. 15
A Woman Is No Man; by Etaf Rum This gorgeous debut follows three generations of Palestinian-American women struggling to express themselves within the confines of their culture as violence rocks their community. It offers an intimate glimpse into a controlling world that feels both foreign and familiar, as well as universal truths about how silence and shame can destroy a family. 7
